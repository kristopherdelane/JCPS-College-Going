What do JCPS students plan to do after graduation?

This chart shows where the students that graduated from JCPS high schools in 2015 indicated they would spend their time post graduation.

Introduction:

Where do JCPS students go when they finish college? According to the 2016 Senior Transition exit survey available from the Kentucky Department of Education School Report Card there are 4 options: College, Workforce, Military, or Other. The data is for the students who graduated in 2015.

College:

The largest destination for JCPS graduates is college with 67% of students who planned to continue to be students. 57% of the graduates planned to attend an in-state post-secondary institution with another 9% who planned to attend out of state institutions. Of the 21 high schools, the top 3 in regards to percentage of population who planned to go to college are DuPont Manual High (92%), Brown School(86%) and Louisville Male(83%). DuPont Manual High also has the highest percentage of students who planned to leave the state to go to college (32%) Only 3 high schools in JCPS have less than half their students planning to attend college including Western High School (46%), The Academy at Shawnee (48%), and Valley High School (49%).



Workforce:

The highest percentage of graduates who planned to enter directly into the workforce were from Western High School (30%) and Valley High School (27%). DuPont Manual High School was the lowest (4%).

Military:

Doss High School had the highest percentage of students who planned to enter the military (14%) followed by Valley High School (13%). The Brown School (2%) and DuPont Manual High School (2%) both had the smallest percentage of students who planned to enter the military.

Other:

Other is a combination of students who indicated they would attend vocational schools, work part time, or 'failed to transition'. The number of students who want to planned to attend vocational school or work part time is very small, less than 50 graduates in each category. 'Failed to transition' is not clearly defined by the data dictionary as what that means or entails for those graduates. The Academy at Shawnee had the highest percentage of students in this category (19%), followed by Iroquois (18%).
